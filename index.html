About

I am a doctoral candidate in History and Philosophy of Science at the University of Pittsburgh. My current research explores epistemic implications of machine learning as it is used in science. I have other ongoing projects on moral and political philosophy of AI, early modern philosophy, and general philosophy of science.

Before coming to Pittsburgh, I completed an MA in Philosophy at Simon Fraser University in Vancouver. Before that, I received my BA from Williams College, where I majored in Computer Science and Philosophy.
